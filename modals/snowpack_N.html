Snowpack supported freshwater resources for much of the Pacific Coast, although a dry summer in the Pacific Northwest somewhat exacerbated water scarcity. By the end of the water year in September 2024, 55% of Washington, Oregon, and Idaho was experiencing at least moderate drought conditions (D1 or higher levels of the U.S. Drought Monitor index). Where water scarcity existed, it generally was not severe: over 95% of the Pacific west experienced moderate drought conditions (D1) or better.
