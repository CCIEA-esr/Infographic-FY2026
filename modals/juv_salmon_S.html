Catches of juvenile coho and Chinook salmon from June surveys in the Northern CCE are indicators of salmon survival during their first few weeks at sea. Catch-per-unit-effort of yearling Chinook salmon in 2025 was once again close to the time series average. Catches of yearling coho salmon were the highest in the 28-year time series and suggest an increasing five-year trend in their early marine survival.


Catch per unit effort of yearling Chinook salmon (top) and yearling coho salmon (bottom) off Oregon and Washington in June, 1998-2025. The blue shaded area is the most recent 5 years of data. Arrows indicate if the recent 5-year trend is positive (↗), neutral (→), or negative (↗). Symbols indicate if the recent 5-year mean is above the upper blue line (+), within the blue lines (↗), or below the lower blue line (−). Data for at-sea juvenile salmon provided by B.
